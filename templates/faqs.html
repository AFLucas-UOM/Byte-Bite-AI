Byte Bite-AI FAQs
1. What is Byte Bite-AI?

Byte Bite-AI is an AI-driven healthy eating assistant designed to provide personalized dietary recommendations whilst dining at the Campus Hub Piazza. Our goal is to make healthy eating easy and accessible for students.

2. How does Byte Bite-AI ensure my privacy?

Byte Bite-AI uses an on-device Rule-Based system to prioritize user privacy. This approach ensures that your data stays on your device, with only insights and model updates being shared, safeguarding your personal information.

3. Which restaurants does Byte Bite-AI support?

Byte Bite-AI currently provides recommendations from several popular dining options at Campus Hub, including Burger King, Pizza Hut, Cafe Cuba, Ottoman Turkish, Boost, Amami, Starbucks and J'Oli.

4. How can I access Byte Bite-AI?

You can access Byte Bite-AI through our website (or app - currently in development). Simply create an account, and you’ll be able to start receiving personalized dining recommendations.

5. Why was Byte Bite-AI created?

Byte Bite-AI was developed as part of the B.Sc. in ICT (Artificial Intelligence) program at the University of Malta, specifically for the study unit course ICS3211: Intelligent Interfaces.

6. Who are the creators of Byte Bite-AI?

The project was created by students Andrea Filiberto Lucas & Sean David Muscat, aiming to combine technology and healthy eating to support students in making better dietary choices.

Healthy Eating FAQs

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a measure that uses your height and weight to estimate whether you are underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. It is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in meters.

To calculate BMI, use the formula: BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m) x height (m)). For example, if you weigh 70 kg and are 1.75 m tall, your BMI would be 22.86.

A balanced diet provides your body with essential nutrients, helps maintain a healthy weight, reduces the risk of chronic diseases, and promotes overall health and well-being.

It is generally recommended to drink at least 2 liters (about 8 cups) of water per day. However, individual needs may vary based on factors such as age, gender, activity level, and climate.

The average kilojoule intake per meal can vary based on individual energy needs, which are influenced by factors such as age, gender, activity level, and the time of day. Generally, a moderately active adult may require around 2,000 to 8,000 KJ per day, with each meal containing roughly 1,500 to 2,500 KJ. Adjusting portion sizes and meal composition can help meet personal energy requirements.
